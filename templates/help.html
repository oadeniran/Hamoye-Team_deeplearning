Features Description

Academic reputation - Looks at the teaching and research quality at the world's universities.

Employer reputation - Assess how institutions prepare students for successful careers, and how the institution provides competent, innovative, and effective graduates.

Faculty/student Score - Assess how institutions provide students with meaningful access to lecturers and tutors.

Citations per faculty - Measures university research quality with a citation per faculty metric, taking the total number of academic citations in papers produced by a university in a five-year period.

International student score - It demonstrates the ability to attract quality students and staff from across the world, and it implies a highly global outlook. Strong international institutions provide a multinational environment, building international sympathies and global awareness.

Four categories based on the institution's provision of programs in the five broad faculty areas used in the university rankings. Due to radically different publication habits and patterns in medicine, an additional category is added based on whether the subject institution has a medical school.
